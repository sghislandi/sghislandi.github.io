Stefano Ghislandi

Postdoctoral Associate at MIT

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, working on neutrinoless double beta decay searches, neutrino physics and astrophysics, low radioactivity techniques, and cryogenic detector development. I am a member of the CUORE and CUPID collaborations, and of the RES-NOVA experiment.

Beyond the main experiments, I am currently working on cryogenic calorimeter R&D for rare-event searches: the ACCESS project on forbidden β-decay spectral shapes, low-background HPGe searches for exotic nuclear decays, development of microwave multiplexing (uMUX) operated with arrays of macro-calorimeters and superconducting nanowires single photon detectors (SNSPDs).

I completed my Ph.D. in Astroparticle Physics at the Gran Sasso Science Institute (2020–2025), and hold a Master’s and Bachelor’s degree in Physics from the Università degli Studi di Milano-Bicocca, both awarded magna cum laude. My Ph.D. work focused on the data-driven background model of the CUORE experiment, for which I developed and currently maintain the CUORE simulation software.
